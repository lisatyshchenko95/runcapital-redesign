1. What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files stored on your device by your browser when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work efficiently and to provide information to site owners about how the site is used.

We also use similar technologies such as local storage and pixel tags. References to “cookies” in this policy include those technologies.

2. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies to:

  • Ensure the website functions correctly (strictly necessary cookies).
  • Measure how visitors use the site so we can improve it (analytics cookies).
  • Remember your preferences, such as cookie-consent choices (preference cookies).

We do not use cookies for advertising or behavioural profiling.

3. Categories of Cookies

Strictly Necessary

These cookies are required for the website to function. They cannot be disabled in our systems. They do not store personally identifiable information.

Analytics

We use [ANALYTICS PROVIDER, e.g. Vercel Analytics / Plausible / GA4] to understand how visitors interact with the site — which pages are most read, how visitors arrive, and where friction exists. Where the provider sets cookies, they are loaded only after you accept them via our cookie banner.

Preferences

Preference cookies remember choices you make, such as your cookie-consent selection, so you are not asked again on every visit.

5. Managing Cookies

You can manage cookies in several ways:

  • Via our cookie banner: accept, reject, or customize your choices on your first visit and re-open the banner at any time from the footer.
  • Via your browser: most browsers allow you to view, delete, or block cookies. Consult your browser’s help documentation.
  • Via industry tools: for analytics opt-outs, refer to the provider’s opt-out links linked above.

Blocking strictly necessary cookies may cause parts of the site to stop working.
